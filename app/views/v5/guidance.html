Guidance

Verifying someone's identity for Companies House

What authorised agents must do to meet the identity verification standard when they verify someone’s identity for Companies House.

There are 2 ways someone can verify their identity for Companies House. They can:

  • verify their identity with Companies House
  • ask you or another authorised agent to verify their identity

If you verify someone's identity, the checks you do must meet Companies House identity verification standard. You can use the following steps to meet the standard.

1. Ask to see identity documents

The person will need to give you a range of identity documents so you can verify their identity.

If they can, they must show you 3 documents in total:

  • one document from group 1
  • 2 different documents from group 1, group 2, or group 3

At least one of the documents must show their current address.

Group 1

Primary identity documents
Document Notes
Passport Any current and valid passport
Biometric residence permit UK
Current photo driving licence (full or provisional) UK, Isle of Man, Channel Islands and EU
Birth certificate - issued within 12 months of birth UK (including those issued by UK authorities overseas), Isle of Man, and Channel Islands
Adoption certificate UK and Channel Islands

If they do not have any documents in group 1

You must ask them to show you:

  • one document from group 2
  • 2 different documents from either group 2 or group 3

At least one of the documents must show the person's current address and date of birth.

If they cannot do this, they must be able to show you 5 documents in total. These must be:

  • a birth certificate issued after the time of birth (UK, Isle of Man and Channel Islands)
  • one document from group 2
  • 3 different documents from group 2 or group 3

At least one of the documents must show the person's current address.

2. Check their identity documents

The documents you check must:

  • be valid, current and original documentation
  • not be a photocopy or scanned copy
  • not be printed from the internet, for example internet bank statements
  • be in the person’s current name
  • only be included once in the document count - for example, 2 banks statements from the same bank will not count as 2 separate documents

Always look for signs of fraud when you check a person’s identity document. The National Document Fraud Unit (NDFU) has guidance on examining identity documents to spot basic forgeries.

3. Keep copies of the documents

You must keep a copy of any documents you use to check a person’s identity while you have a working relationship and for 5 years after.

You do not need to upload copies of the documents when you tell us you have verified someone’s identity. However, we may ask for more information on identity checks later if we need to.

4. Check their address history

The person you are verifying will need to give you their address history for the last 5 years. Where possible, you should ask to see documentation to confirm the address history.

5. Check if they have changed their name

You must ask the person about any previous name changes. You should ask them to show you documentation to support any changes of name.

If they cannot show you proof of the name change, you should find out why and consider whether to verify their identity.

You can usually accept documents which show the person’s name as a synonym. For example, a document might say the person’s name is Samantha on their passport, but Sam on their bank card.

What happens when you verify someone’s identity

When you have successfully verified someone’s identity, we’ll send the person a Companies House personal code. This is a code that connects a person’s verified identity to their Companies House records.

If you can give an email address for the person, we can send their code by email. This is the safest, fastest way for us to send the personal code.

If the person does not have an email, we will need to send it to their home address.

What we’ll show on the public register

We will show that you have verified the person’s identity as part of any records about them on the public register. We’ll show:

  • the name of your authorised agent, or your own name if you are a sole trader
  • the date you verified their identity
  • which documents you checked to verify their identity
  • the names of all supervisory bodies your business is registered with

What to do if you cannot verify someone’s identity

The person will need to get documents to be able to verify their identity for Companies House.

If you cannot file annual accounts because you need more time to verify someone’s identity, you can apply to extend the accounts filing deadline. You must do this before the normal filing deadline.
