Who can use this service

This service is for Companies House authorised agents to tell us when they have verified someone’s identity.

You, or the business you work for, must be registered as a Companies House authorised agent before you can use this service.

What is a Companies House authorised agent?

Authorised agents are businesses that are registered with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. For example, accountants, solicitors or company formation agents.

They must register with us to be able to:

  • form companies and registrable partnerships on behalf of clients
  • file on behalf of clients
  • verify the identity of clients for Companies House

For more information, read the guidance on how to register as a Companies House authorised agent.

Before you start

You must have completed checks to verify the person’s identity. The checks you do to verify someone’s identity for Companies House must meet the identity verification standard, in Registrar’s rules.

Read the guidance on how to meet Companies House identity verification standard before you start.

You must keep records of the checks you do to verify someone’s identity – we may ask for more information if we need to. If you cannot provide records of identity checks when asked, you may be suspended or deauthorised from acting as a CH authorised agent.

You also need to check your authorised agent details are correct. You must tell us if there have been any changes to the agent’s Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration details before you use this service.

What you’ll need

When you have completed checks to verify someone’s identity, you will need to use this service to tell us the:

  • person’s name
  • person’s date of birth
  • person’s home address
  • person's email address
  • date you completed the identity checks
  • documents you used as evidence to verify their identity

You’ll also need to complete a declaration to confirm that the person is who they claim to be and you have completed checks that meet the Companies House identity verification standard.

What happens when you verify someone’s identity

If your checks meet the Companies House standard of identity verification, we will email the person their Companies House personal code. This is an 11 character code that is given to a person once they’ve verified their identity. 

They will need to use their Companies House personal code to connect their verified identity to our records. This is so that we know the correct person is linked to the name we have on file.

They can share this code with people who file on their behalf, or for their company.

What we’ll show on the public register

Once the person has used their personal code to connect their verified identity to our records, we will show that they are verified.

As part of any records about the person, we will show:

  • the name of your authorised agent
  • the names of the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory bodies the agent is registered with
  • the date you completed the identity checks
