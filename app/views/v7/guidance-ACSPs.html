Tell Companies House you have verified someone's identity

Who can use this service

This service is for Companies House authorised agents to tell us when they have verified someone’s identity. Authorised agents are also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs).

What is a Companies House authorised agent?

Authorised agents are businesses that are registered with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. For example, accountants, solicitors or company formation agents.

They must register with us to be able to:

  • form companies and registrable partnerships on behalf of clients
  • file on behalf of clients
  • verify the identity of clients for Companies House

You, or the business you work for, must register as a Companies House authorised agent before you can use this service.

Before you start

You must have completed identity checks that meet the Companies House identity verification standard.

Read the guidance on how to meet Companies House identity verification standard.

What you’ll need

When you have completed checks to verify someone’s identity, you will need to use this service to tell us the:

  • person’s name
  • person’s date of birth
  • person’s home address
  • person's email address
  • date you completed the identity checks
  • documents you used as evidence to verify their identity

You’ll also need to complete a declaration to confirm that:

  • the person is who they claim to be
  • you have completed checks that meet the Companies House identity verification standard
You do not need to submit copies of the documents you used to verify someone's identity. However, you must keep copies of the documents for 7 years.

What happens when you verify someone’s identity

We will email the person their Companies House personal code. This is an 11 character code that is given to a person once they’ve verified their identity. 

They should only share this code with people who file on their behalf, or for their company.

When they'll need their personal code

They will need to use this to connect their verified identity to our records. This is so that we know the correct person is linked to the name we have on file.

They'll need the personal code for various reasons. For example:

  • as part of some company filings, such as the confirmation statement
  • if they are appointed as a director
  • if they become a person with significant control (PSC)

We may ask for more information about the identity checks you completed if we need to. You must provide evidence of the identity checks you completed if we ask you.

You may be suspended or removed from the Companies House list of authorised agents if:

  • you cannot provide records of identity checks when asked
  • you have not met our identity verification standard

What we’ll show on the public register

Once the person has used their personal code to connect their verified identity to our records, we will show that they are verified.

As part of any records about the person, we will show:

  • the name of your authorised agent
  • the names of the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory bodies the agent is registered with
  • the date you completed the identity checks
