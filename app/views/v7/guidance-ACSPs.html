What this service is for

From XX DATE, people will need to verify their identity for Companies House if they are (any of the following):

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers
  • someone who files for a company

Use this service to tell Companies House you have verified someone’s identity.

Who can use this service

You must be a Companies House authorised agent to use this service. This is also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP).

Individuals or organisations, such as accountants or solicitors, that are registered with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body can apply to register as an authorised agent.

How to verify someone’s identity

There are 2 ways someone can verify their identity for Companies House. They can:

  • verify their identity with Companies House using GOV.UK One Login
  • ask an authorised agent to verify their identity

If you're verifying someone’s identity for Companies House, you must complete checks that meet the identity verification standard.

What information you need to submit

You'll need to enter the person's details as they are shown on the documents you used to check their identity. You’ll need to tell us their:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • home address

You do not need to upload copies of the documents. However, you must keep a copy of any documents you use to check a person’s identity while you have a working relationship and for 5 years after.

You’ll also need to tell us the date you completed the identity checks.

You should provide an email address for the person if they have one. This must belong to the person and cannot be an email address for a shared inbox.

What happens after you verify someone’s identity

When you have successfully verified someone’s identity, we’ll send the person their Companies House personal code. This is a code that connects a person’s verified identity to their Companies House records.

If you give an email address for the person, we can send their code by email. If not, we will send the code to their home address by post.

What we’ll show on the public register

We will show that you have verified the person’s identity as part of any records about them on the public register.

We'll show:

  • the name of the authorised agent
  • the date you verified their identity
  • the names of all supervisory bodies your business is registered with
