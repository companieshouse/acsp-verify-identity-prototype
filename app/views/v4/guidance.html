Guidance

Companies House identity verification standard

Contents

  1. What you must do to check someone's identity
  2. Routes to check a person's identity
  3. Identity documents to check

Companies House verification standard outlines identity checks authorised agents can do to meet the medium confidence level from the Good Practice Guide (GPG) 45.

Authorised agents will need to do identity checks that meet this standard if they want to verify the identity of people for the Companies House register.

What you must do to check someone's identity

The person will need to give you a range of identity documents so you can verify their identity.

You can use one of 3 routes to meet Companies House identity verification standard. The documents you need to check will depend on the identity checking route you use.

You must try to use route 1 first if your client can provide the documents needed for this.

The documents you check must:

  • be valid, current and original documentation
  • not be a photocopy or scanned copy
  • not be printed from the internet, for example internet bank statements
  • be in the person’s current name
  • only be included once in the document count - for example, 2 banks statements from the same bank will not count as 2 separate documents

Check their address history

The person you are verifying will need to give you their address history for the last 5 years. Where possible, you should ask to see documentation to confirm the address history.

Check if they have changed their name

You must ask the person about any previous name changes. They will need to show you documentation to support any changes of name.

If they cannot show you proof of the name change, you should find out why and consider whether to verify their identity.

You can usually accept the documents which show the person’s name as a synonym. For example, a document might say the person’s name is Samantha on their passport, but Sam on their bank card.

How to check documents

Always look for signs of fraud when you check a person’s identity document. The National Document Fraud Unit (NDFU) has guidance on examining identity documents to spot basic forgeries.

Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) ID checking guidelines includes information on how to check identity documents, including:

  • driving licences
  • passports
  • birth certificates
  • EEA photo identity cards
  • HM Forces ID card
  • firearms licences
  • biometric residence permits

Routes to check a person’s identity

Route 1

The person must be able to show:

  • one document from group 1
  • two different documents from either group 1, group 2, or group 3

At least one of the documents must show the applicant’s current address.

Route 2

If the person doesn’t have any of the documents in group 1, then they must be able to show:

  • one document from group 2
  • 2 different documents from either group 2 or group 3

At least one of the documents must show the applicant’s current address and date of birth.

Route 3

Route 3 can only be used if it’s impossible to process the application through routes 1 or 2.

For route 3, the person must be able to show:

  • a birth certificate issued after the time of birth (UK, Isle of Man and Channel Islands)
  • one document from group 2
  • 3 different documents from group 2 or group 3

At least one of the documents must show the applicant’s current address.

Identity documents

Group 1: Primary identity documents

Document Notes
Passport Any current and valid passport
Biometric residence permit UK
Current driving licence photocard - (full or provisional) UK, Isle of Man, Channel Islands and EU
Birth certificate - issued within 12 months of birth UK, Isle of Man, and Channel Islands - including those issued by UK authorities overseas, for example embassies, High Commissions and HM Forces
Adoption certificate UK and Channel Islands

Group 2: Trusted government documents

Document Notes
Current driving licence photocard - (full or provisional) All countries outside the UK (excluding Isle of Man and Channel Islands)
Current driving licence (full or provisional) - paper version (if issued before 1998) UK, Isle of Man, and Channel Islands
Birth certificate - issued after time of birth UK, Isle of Man, and Channel Islands
Marriage/civil partnership certificate UK and Channel Islands
Immigration document, visa, or work permit Issued by a country outside the UK
HM Forces ID card UK
Firearms licence UK, Isle of Man, Channel Islands and Isle of Man
