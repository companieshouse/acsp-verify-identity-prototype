Reverify someone’s identity for Companies House

Use this service to tell us if you’ve completed checks to reverify a person’s identity. You only need to this if the person has received a reverification notice from us.

The reverification notices we send can include instructions on how a person needs to reverify. For example, we may have told them which identity documents they can or cannot use.

The identity checks you do to reverify a person must:

  • meet our identity verification standard
  • follow any instructions included in the person’s reverification notice

We'll ask you to confirm this, and that you are satisfied the person is who they claim to be.

What you'll need

You’ll need to give details from the person’s original verification so we can update their existing verification record. You’ll need their:

  • personal code
  • email address

You'll also need to give details from the reverification checks you completed. You'll need to tell us:

  • the person's full name
  • the person's date of birth
  • the person's home address
  • how you checked their identity documents
  • which documents they provided
  • the document references, expiry dates and country of issue (if relevant)
  • the date you completed the reverification checks

It takes around 10 minutes to use this service.

What happens when you reverify someone's identity

We will:

  • email the person to tell them you have reverified their identity
  • update their identity verification statement with the new details you provide

If the person connects their verified identity to their Companies House record as a director or person with significant control (PSC), we’ll show the identity reverification statement on the public register to confirm they are reverified.

Read the guidance about verifying someone's identity for Companies House to find out what information the statement will include.
