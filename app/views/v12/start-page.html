Tell Companies House you have verified someone’s identity

Use this service to tell us when you've verified someone's identity for Companies House.

Once you've verified their identity, we'll send their Companies House personal code to the email address you provide for them. This code can be used each time the person needs to confirm they're verified.

Read the guidance about Companies House personal codes to find out more.

What you need to do to verify someone’s identity

Our identity verification standard sets out the documents you can use and the checks you need to do to verify someone's identity for Comapnies House. Read our guidance on how to meet the identity verification standard.

You must keep records of the identity checks you complete, including copies of the documents. We may ask to see these records.

! Warning We could suspend or stop your business from acting as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) if you have not met our identity verification standard.

What you'll need

You’ll need to tell us the person’s:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • home address
  • email address - the email address you give can only be used once to verify someone’s identity

You’ll also need to tell us about the documents you used to verify their identity and how they were checked. You’ll need to tell us:

  • if the documents were checked by a person, or using technology to validate the cryptographic features of the document
  • which documents the person provided
  • a document reference, expiry date and country of issue (for the documents that have them)
  • the date you completed the identity checks

It takes around 10 minutes to use this service.

What we’ll show on the public register

When you verify someone’s identity, we'll ask you to confirm an identity verification statement to say you are satisfied the person is who they claim to be.

If the person connects their verified identity to their Companies House record as a director or person with significant control (PSC), we’ll show the statement on the public register to confirm they are verified.

Read the guidance about verifying someone's identity for Companies House to find out what information the statement will include.
